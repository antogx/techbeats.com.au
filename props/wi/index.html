1

Offer Acceptance to Contract

13 steps · 15 checklist items
▾
What to do
  1. Confirm the offer acceptance details with the sales agent and buyer.
  2. The contract is normally issued within one to two days after offer acceptance.
  3. If the contract has not been received, contact the sales agent and follow up.
  4. If the contract is sent directly to the buyer, ask the buyer to forward it to their conveyancer for review.
  5. Confirm that the conveyancer has received the contract.
  6. Review the contract and conveyancer’s advice to identify all key dates, including the contract date, deposit due date, building and pest date, finance date and settlement date.
  7. Update the settlement tracker accurately with all relevant dates and contract details.
  8. Ask another team member to peer review the settlement tracker and confirm that the dates match the contract.
  9. Once the conveyancer confirms that the contract is suitable to sign, follow up with the buyer to complete signing.
  10. Confirm that the signed contract has been returned to the sales agent or conveyancer.
  11. Follow up with the sales agent until the vendor has signed the contract.
  12. Once both parties have signed, update the tracker to show that the contract has been executed.
  13. The building and pest inspection process can be started while the contract is being reviewed and signed, where appropriate.

Building and Pest Inspection

13 steps · 15 checklist items · 1 example
▾
What to do
  1. Confirm whether the buyer will arrange the building and pest inspection.
  2. If the buyer wants us to arrange it:
  3. Confirm the inspection clause expiry date in the contract.
  4. Ask the sales agent whether they have a recommended local inspector who can complete the inspection before the deadline.
  5. Search for qualified local building and pest inspectors.
  6. Confirm the inspector’s availability, pricing and expected report delivery time.
  7. Verify that the inspector holds the required licence, accreditation and insurance.
  8. Provide the inspector’s details and quotation to the buyer.
  9. Obtain the buyer’s approval before confirming the inspection.
  10. Introduce the buyer, inspector and sales agent by email.
  11. Confirm that the inspection has been booked within the contract deadline.
  12. Follow up until the building and pest reports are received.
  13. Save the reports and notify the responsible team member of any major defects or concerns.

3

Finance Process

12 steps · 13 checklist items
▾
What to do
  1. Confirm the finance approval deadline stated in the contract.
  2. Check with the buyer which lender or mortgage broker is handling the application.
  3. Ask the buyer for an update on the finance application and whether any documents are still outstanding.
  4. Confirm whether the lender has completed the property valuation.
  5. Follow up with the buyer before the finance deadline to confirm whether formal approval is expected on time.
  6. Advise the buyer not to treat the loan as approved until they receive formal written approval from the lender or broker.
  7. Ask the buyer to send the formal finance approval letter to their conveyancer as soon as it is received.
  8. Confirm with the buyer that the conveyancer has received the approval letter.
  9. If finance is delayed, inform the conveyancer as early as possible so they can request an extension of the finance date.
  10. Confirm that any finance extension has been approved by the vendor and documented in writing.
  11. Escalate any declined application, valuation shortfall or major lending issue immediately.
  12. Update the tracker once finance is formally approved.

4

Tenancy Process

16 steps · 18 checklist items · 1 example
▾
What to do
  1. Confirm whether the property is already tenanted and obtain the current lease, rental ledger and property management details if applicable.
  2. Start the tenancy process once the contract becomes unconditional, unless the buyer has already appointed a property manager.
  3. Ask the sales agent for the current rental appraisal and details of any recommended local property managers.
  4. Contact other established property management agencies in the area and obtain independent rental appraisals.
  5. Confirm each property manager’s estimated weekly rent, management fees, letting fees, marketing costs, inspection frequency and service inclusions.
  6. Check the property manager’s experience in the suburb and with similar investment properties.
  7. Provide suitable property management options and rental appraisals to the buyer.
  8. Advise the buyer to speak with the shortlisted property managers and select their preferred agency.
  9. Where possible, arrange for shortlisted property managers to inspect the property after the contract becomes unconditional and before settlement.
  10. Coordinate access with the sales agent, vendor or current property manager.
  11. Confirm whether any repairs, cleaning, safety checks or compliance work are recommended before advertising the property.
  12. Ask the buyer to sign the property management agreement directly with the selected property manager.
  13. Provide the selected property manager with the contract details, settlement date, buyer details and relevant property information.
  14. If the property is vacant, confirm the proposed advertising date, inspection schedule and tenant selection process.
  15. If the property is already tenanted, coordinate the transfer of management records, keys, lease documents, bond details and tenant information.
  16. Follow up until the property manager confirms that the tenancy process or management transfer is underway."

Pre-Settlement Inspection

9 steps · 12 checklist items · 1 example
▾
What to do
  1. Contact the sales agent and schedule the pre-settlement inspection before settlement.
  2. Confirm who will attend the inspection on behalf of the buyer.
  3. Ask the sales agent to ensure access to all rooms, garages, sheds and included fixtures.
  4. Check that the property is in the same condition as when the contract was signed.
  5. Confirm that agreed repairs, cleaning or removal of items have been completed.
  6. Test the main appliances, lights, taps, toilets, hot water, heating and cooling where possible.
  7. Check that no new damage has occurred and that all included items remain at the property.
  8. Take photos or a video during the inspection where possible.
  9. If the buyer cannot attend, ask the sales agent or property manager to provide a video walkthrough.
